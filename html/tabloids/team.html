Core Team

jamil zaki, phd

President of the Board | Co-Founder

@jazzmule | jamil@thepeoplesscience.org

Jamil received his BA from Boston University, his Ph.D. from Columbia University, and postdoctoral training at Harvard University. He is an Assistant Professor of Psychology at Stanford University and directs the Stanford Social Neuroscience Laboratory. The People's Science is an idea rooted in his belief that science should belong to everyone, and that a platform should exist where people can learn from, contribute to, and engage with the scientific community directly.

As a lifetime East Coaster recently transplanted to California, his hobbies include compulsive hiking, eating avocados, and trying to remember how to drive again.

stephanie fine sasse, edm

Executive Director | Co-Founder

@thescientish | steph@thepeoplesscience.org

Stephanie received her BS in General Science + Psychology at Portland State University and her Ed.M. from Harvard University, where she was selected as the 2013 Leadership in Education Scholarship recipient. In addition to her work in neuroimaging and psychology research at Harvard University, she has designed educational exhibits to teach the public about the brain at Oregon Health & Science University, taught and developed scientific literacy curriculum at Harvard Graduate School of Education, Harvard Faculty of Arts & Sciences, and Portland State University, and worked as a fundraiser, project manager, and volunteer coordinator for a number of non-profit organizations in Portland, Oregon. Harnessing these skills, she leads The People's Science's effort to build a centralized, collaborative hub that provides opportunities for researchers to share their work, trains and empowers the public to engage with the scientific community and findings, and provides tools for everyone to put science to work.

Her hobbies include themed parties, planning scavenger hunts in foreign cities, color coding everything, and sleeping in.

maya bialik, edm

Associate Director | Co-Founder

@mayabialik | maya@thepeoplesscience.org

Maya received her B.S.'s in Psychology and Biology and B.A. in Linguistics at the University of Massachusetts at Amherst in 2011 and her Ed.M. in Mind, Brain, and Education at Harvard University in 2012. She is currently a freelance writer, editor, and research synthesizer who works closely with the Center for Curriculum Redesign and ImprovScience. She has done research in developmental neuroscience, neurobiology, psycholinguistics, and science education. She has worked to apply findings from Cognitive Science to Education in various non-profits including a cultural exchange program in Beijing, China and a hands-on STEM program. She leads workshops on science communication, improvisation, and interdisciplinarity and shares her talents with The People's Science by assisting with the development of our training, communication, and outreach projects.

Her hobbies include photography, hoarding books, synchronized swimming, and putting sour cream on everything.

Andrew Watson

Partnership & Collaboration Manager | Senior

Curriculum Developer andrew@translatethebrain.com

Andrew received his M. Ed. in Mind Brain Education from Harvard University Graduate School of Education in 2012, his M.A. in English Language and Literature from Boston University. (1997), and his A.B. from Harvard College in Medieval History and Literature. (1988). He’s taught for 13 years at Loomis Chaffee, four years at Concord Academy, and two summers at Phillips Exeter Summer School. Like most people who work in schools, he’s done some of everything: from teaching English to 9th, 10th, 11th, and 12 graders, advising students, directing plays and teaching acting classes, to coordinating segments of the community service program, interviewing for admission office, and living and working in dormitories.

He also served for four years as Dean of Faculty at Loomis Chaffee. He now runs his own consulting organization, Translate The Brain, helping educators leverage an understanding of the brain to improve their teaching.
